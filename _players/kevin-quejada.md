  Kevin Quejada | 23-year-old centre-forward | Colombia

He frequently drops to invite link-up play, and when not found, reacts quickly with sharp turns and well-timed runs into tight spaces behind the defensive line. In more open situations, he uses his pace effectively to attack space, making him a constant threat in behind. Combined with his strength, he is extremely difficult to contain.

  His standout quality is his positioning inside the box. Quejada consistently finds himself in dangerous areas, showing strong instincts as a true fox in the box.   Areas for improvement

  While effective in attacking situations, his first touch can be more consistent, particularly under pressure. His passing in the final third, especially in link-up play, can also improve. Given his physical profile, there is clear potential to become more dominant in aerial duels, particularly when contesting long balls.

  Career trajectory and current form

  Quejada developed at Club Fútbol Paz, an amateur club in Colombia, before moving to Grêmio’s academy in Brazil in 2020. He made his senior debut for Grêmio in Serie B in 2022 and later gained experience through loan spells, including Barracas Central in the Argentine top division and Llaneros FC in Colombia’s Primera B, where he established himself and helped the club secure promotion to Primera A.

  In September 2024, he moved to CD Feirense in Portugal. Limited opportunities there led to a return to South America, where he helped Cúcuta Deportivo secure promotion to Primera A in 2025.

  In January 2026, he joined Estudiantes de Mérida in the Venezuelan top tier (Liga FUTVE) on loan. There, he has hit outstanding form, delivering 9 goals and 1 assist in just 10 matches and establishing himself as one of the league’s most dangerous forwards.

  Transfer outlook

  While the Venezuelan league is the lowest-ranked competition in CONMEBOL, consistent goalscoring remains highly valued. Quejada’s profile as a powerful, mobile striker with strong box instincts makes him an attractive option for clubs looking for a traditional number nine.

  A return to the Portuguese second tier remains a possibility, but his profile also fits well with Scandinavian football, where physical forwards with strong finishing instincts are in demand. With a contract reportedly running until 31.12.2028, he could still represent a cost-effective opportunity for clubs seeking a proven goal scorer with upside.
